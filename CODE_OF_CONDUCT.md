# Code of Conduct

## Repository Scope

This is an independent community policy for `beamer-up`, an unofficial Beamer theme initiative for the University of the Philippines System and an independently maintained derivative work originally based on `beamer-uu`, distributed here under the LaTeX Project Public License `LPPL 1.3c`.
Community governance for this repository is owned by its maintainer, Zildjian E. California, and does not speak for the original `beamer-uu` author, Utrecht University, or the University of the Philippines System unless explicitly stated.
It draws inspiration from publicly available University of the Philippines values documents, but it does not imply UP affiliation, adoption, sponsorship, or endorsement unless repository maintainers explicitly say so.

## Our Commitment

We are committed to building a respectful, harassment-free, inclusive, and quality-focused collaboration environment.
We aim to foster:

- leadership through transparent and accountable participation
- excellence and continuous improvement
- ethical and professional conduct
- expertise through disciplined and well-documented practice
- inquiry, learning, and evidence-based discussion
- service-oriented and socially responsible work
- diversity, equity, and inclusion
- meaningful collaboration and shared accountability
- sustainability in long-term project decisions

## Expected Behavior

- Communicate respectfully, clearly, and in good faith.
- Focus feedback on ideas, code, design choices, and outcomes, not personal attacks.
- Uphold integrity in authorship, attribution, citation, testing, and reporting.
- Welcome diverse perspectives and remove barriers to participation.
- Share knowledge, mentor when possible, and collaborate constructively.
- Respect `LPPL 1.3c` obligations, including attribution, clear change notices for derivative work, and accurate statements about support, maintenance, or endorsement.
- Distinguish `beamer-up` clearly from the original `beamer-uu` work when documenting, packaging, or discussing releases.
- Respect branding and redistribution constraints for institutional names, logos, and other bundled assets.
- Respect confidentiality, privacy, and responsible data handling.

## Unacceptable Behavior

- Harassment, hate speech, discrimination, or demeaning conduct.
- Bullying, intimidation, threats, or sustained disruption.
- Doxxing or sharing private information without explicit consent.
- Sexualized language or unwelcome sexual attention.
- Misrepresentation, plagiarism, fabrication, or intentional deception.
- Removing or obscuring attribution, license context, or modification notices that recipients need to distinguish derivative work from upstream material.
- Representing `beamer-up` as if it were the original `beamer-uu` release, an upstream-supported continuation, or a Utrecht University publication.
- Misrepresentation of affiliation, endorsement, authorship, or institutional approval.
- Retaliation against people who raise good-faith concerns.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies to all project spaces, including issues, pull requests, discussions, reviews, documentation, screenshots, demo assets, and events where project participation is represented.

## Reporting

Report concerns to `zecalifornia@up.edu.ph`.
Include relevant links, screenshots, logs, and context when possible.
Good-faith reports are handled as confidentially as practical.

## Enforcement

Maintainers may take any action appropriate to protect the community and project, including content moderation, warnings, temporary restrictions, or permanent bans.
Enforcement decisions prioritize community safety, fairness, and proportionality.
